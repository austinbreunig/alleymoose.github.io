About the Series

Alley Moose consists of two virtual races every month between May and October, where competitors have the entire month to complete the routes.

A trail, or mountain bike, route and a gravel route will be published each month. Competitors are encouraged to do both disciplines, but are free to ride whichever routes they like.

A Grand Depart will take place each month for those looking to ride with others. Otherwise, riders have the flexibility to complete the routes whenever convenient.

These events are free. The only requirement is that you record your ride with GPS and upload it using a service like Garmin or Strava.

Our routes are meant to be challenging and a bit adventurous, introducing riders to some less-traveled ways in and around Boulder County.

Guidelines

This is a fun event, but riding bikes can be serious business. Ride safely. Wear a helmet, be respectful to other riders, and know your limits.

These races are modeled on the principles of self-supported racing. This means completing every mile of the route under your own power: no support crews, no gear sharing, no planned outside help, and Leave No Trace.
